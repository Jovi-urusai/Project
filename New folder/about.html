  1. CPR

    2. Training is a practical activity where individuals learn and practice techniques to manually maintain circulation and breathing in someone experiencing cardiac arrest or respiratory failure. This training often includes hands-on demonstrations of cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR), the use of automated external defibrillators (AEDs), and other life-saving interventions. Participants are taught to recognize the signs of cardiac arrest and the importance of acting quickly to improve the chances of survival. Additionally, training sessions may cover the proper techniques for chest compressions and rescue breaths, emphasizing the correct depth and rate to maximize effectiveness.
